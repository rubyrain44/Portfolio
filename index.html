Intro

Hi! My name is Lorraine and I have been working in the retail field with focuses in management and visual merchandising, but I have always wondered what my calling in life was. I knew I had finally found the answer with coding. I have always been a creative person who loves to help others and with coding I am able to put both of those qualities to use by building fun, interactive, and helpful applications for users. There are countless possibilities and endless opportunities to better the lives and world around us and it all starts with a blank html page and idea.

I graduated with a Certificate of Achievement from the online bootcamp Coding Dojo in each Python, MERN, and Java as well as competencies in web fundamentals and Projects & Algorithms.

I look forward to finding my place in this world where I can continue to challenge myself and grow within the field to become a better developer and person.

Work

You can visit my Github account to follow what I am currently up to and building as well as scoping out past projects that I have completed and assignments from my time at Coding Dojo. I continue to code and build in my free time, trying to build and grow my portfolio and skills.

Featured Projects:

  • What's For Dinner? (Python) - I feel like everyone at one point faces the struggle to know what they want for dinner and many an arguement has been had over the debate. I thought it would be fun to build a website where users can create an account and add dinners to the database. Then, when someone needs to know what to eat for dinner they can select the button on the main landing page of the website and it will generate a random entry from the collection for the user. I think it offers a fun social aspect as well as hopefully being a useful tool for those stressful nights where you can't make the decision for dinner on your own or together.

  • Thoughts & Confessions (Java) - This was my solo project that I built for my Projects & Algorithms exam. It was an idea for a social media type website that held an anonymous aspect to the concept. Once a user creates an account they are able to log into the website and choose between either a Thoughts or Confession tab. They are then taken to that dashboard where they will see the full database of information pertaining to the choice they made. What makes this idea different from msot social media websites is that it will not share the username of the poster. I felt keeping that anonymous allowed users to share whatever they wanted to without the worry of judgement or having to publicly admit whatever it was they wanted to get off their chest & mind. It's meant to be a safe place to share with the community and be interactive with like and comment features.
